History of Amanokrom Gyaseman

Our Royal Heritage

Amanokrom Gyaseman traces its origins to noble ancestors whose leadership established unity, governance, and cultural strength.

The palace remains the traditional seat of authority, preserving sacred customs and guiding community development.

Through generations, chiefs and elders have ensured peace, justice, and prosperity among the people.

Culture & Traditions

Festivals

Annual festivals celebrate ancestry, unity, and gratitude. Chiefs, elders, and youth gather in colorful traditional display.

Traditional Attire

Rich kente cloth and symbolic ornaments represent royalty, dignity, and cultural identity.

Drumming & Dance

Traditional drumming and dance express history, spirituality, and communal harmony.

Royal Leadership

The Chief

Custodian of the land and supreme traditional authority.

The Chief of Amanokrom Gyaseman serves as the spiritual, cultural, and administrative head of the community. He presides over traditional councils, resolves disputes, safeguards ancestral lands, and ensures the preservation of customs and values.

His leadership promotes unity, peace, and development, working closely with elders and subchiefs to advance the welfare of the people.

The Queenmother

Advisor and mother of the community.

The Queenmother plays a vital advisory role within the traditional council. She oversees matters concerning women, children, and family welfare.

She also participates in selecting and advising the Chief, ensuring leadership upholds moral standards, tradition, and community harmony.

Subchiefs & Their Roles

Okyeame (Linguist)

Spokesperson of the Chief who communicates messages and preserves proverbs.

Gyaasehene

Oversees palace administration and daily traditional affairs.

Krontihene

Leads the youth and assists in maintaining order and security.
